About The Project

Reducing electronic waste in student projects

This capstone project represents the culmination of my studies in UX design, research, and writing. Over the course of 6 months, I identified a real-world problem affecting CU Boulder Students and developed a comprehensive solution through user-centered design methodologies.

The project began with extensive user research to understand pain points and opportunities, followed by iterative design processes including wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing. The final deliverables include a webiste that helps students figure out how to reduce their waste when building projects, and graphic design media campaign.

Through this project, I demonstrated my ability to conduct user research, synthesizing findings, creating design solutions, and lead a team. Poster made by Nita

My UX Process

Research & Discovery

User Research

To understand the needs and behaviors of our target users, I conducted:

  • 10 in-depth user interviews
  • Contextual inquiry sessions
  • Resource gathering
  • Engineering mindset with projects

Key findings revealed that students struggled with having time and energy to care about the enviroment and desired creating a project that outlasted them.

Competitive Analysis

I evaluated 8 engineering disciplines:

  • Everyone follows the design proccess
  • Dveryone follows proffessors rubrics
  • No one knows where to go for needed resources

This analysis informed our design strategy by designing around the design system and giving many resources.

Key Insights

Through synthesis of research data, I identified three primary insights:

  • Users needed locations for renewable resources but existing solutions didn't address it well
  • fatigued casued pollution was more common than expected
  • Users valued sustainability more above other considerations

These insights directly shaped our design priorities.

Project Outcomes & Reflection

This capstone project resulted in learning about how to work with users, try and find root causes, ux writing, ux research,nd a ux design. The process deepened my understanding of the engineering mindest and demonstrated my ability to lessen the burden sustainability has during intense working time.

Key takeaways from this experience include foreplanning is key in sustainability and working with institutions is long and time consuming. Moving forward, I would try and work with institutions in advance and try and gather many different stakeholders into one room. This project has prepared me for future UX work.
